## 2025-03-05 - [Plaintext Credit Card Exposure and Unsecured CVV Input]
**Vulnerability:** Full credit card numbers were displayed in plaintext on the final checkout review page, and CVV inputs were left completely unmasked (visible in plaintext to shoulder surfers) and uncontrolled. Additionally, there was zero validation on step progression, allowing users to submit empty/malformed payment information.
**Learning:** Developers often focus on styling and basic workflow, but overlook basic data privacy standards (like PCI-DSS guidelines) on presentational elements. Relying solely on client-side visual indicators (like "required" HTML5 flags) fails when forms aren't wrapped in traditional form submission handlers.
**Prevention:** Always mask sensitive inputs (CVV must use `type="password"`) and obfuscate card numbers to display only the last 4 digits on any review screens. Always implement explicit procedural validation on step-by-step navigation to ensure required structures are checked before proceeding.
